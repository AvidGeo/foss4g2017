The importance of answering this call

We need your ideas, talents and insights for the best possible conference.

Ultimately, the quality of a conference is a function of the people who are presenting and the content they are sharing. At FOSS4G, that content comes from you the people who make and use FOSS4G software and the incredible sharing culture that surrounds this community.

Process

Timeline
Tues, March 31: Call for papers closes
March 27: Community voting opens
April 10: Community voting close
May 1: Accepted papers announced

We expect to have room for 200-250 talks selected through this Call for Presentations. Keynote and invited presentations will round out the progam. The volunteer FOSS4G Program Committee will lead the process but you, the attendees will help select the program through an open “Community Voting” process. The top vote getters from Community Voting will be included in the program and the Program Committee will select the remainder of the program to provide a diversity of voices and content.

We are looking for a diverse array of presentations

The Program Committee is looking for a variety of different types of stories across a broad range of sectors and industries. During the Call for Presentation registration process you will be asked tag your abstract in the following ways:

Type of Story

  • FOSS4G project updates and development, the traditional cornerstone of this conference
  • Stories about the adoption of FOSS4G technology within business, government and academia
  • Stories about use cases and applications that are powered by FOSS4G technology
  • Research that involves and employs FOSS4G tech
  • Research that involves and employs FOSS4G tech
  • Lessons learned from adopting & using FOSS4G tools

Theme

  • FOSS4G Development
        updates on existing projects, new FOSS4G tool/projects
  • New & Related Technologies
        drones, R, IoT, connectors, satellite data, machine learning
  • Education
        research, curriculum, K-12
  • Business
        products powered by FOSS4G, business adoption
  • Government & Policy
        local, state, federal agency use; policy and planning support
  • Sciences & Related Topics
        climate, natural resources, environment, logistics, analytics
